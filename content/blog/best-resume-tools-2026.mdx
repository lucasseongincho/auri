## What to Look for in a Resume Tool

Before the comparisons, it's worth establishing the criteria. A resume tool
should help you with some combination of:

- **Building and formatting** — creating a clean, ATS-safe resume
- **Keyword optimization** — matching your resume to job descriptions
- **Content improvement** — rewriting weak bullet points into strong ones
- **Application support** — cover letters, LinkedIn, and beyond

Most tools do one or two of these well. Few do all of them.

## The Tools

### Jobscan

**Best for:** ATS keyword analysis specifically

Jobscan is the most established name in this category. It compares your
resume to a job description and gives you a match score along with a
list of missing keywords.

What it does well: keyword gap analysis, formatting compatibility checks,
LinkedIn optimization (on paid plan).

What it doesn't do: write or rewrite your resume, generate cover letters,
help with interview prep or strategy.

**Pricing:** Free plan (limited scans), $49.95/month for Pro.

**Bottom line:** A solid tool if keyword optimization is your only gap.
Expensive relative to what you get if your needs are broader.

---

### Resume.io

**Best for:** Creating a polished resume from scratch quickly

Resume.io is a resume builder with a wide selection of templates and a
guided fill-in-the-blank experience. It's designed for speed — you can
have a decent-looking resume in 20 minutes.

What it does well: template variety, ease of use, clean exports.

What it doesn't do: ATS analysis, content improvement, cover letters
beyond basic templates, or any AI-powered optimization.

**Pricing:** Free plan (limited exports), ~$24.95/month for full access.

**Bottom line:** Good for getting a visually clean resume quickly.
Not a tool for optimization or strategy.

---

### Teal

**Best for:** Job search organization and tracking

Teal is positioned as a full job search platform. It includes a resume
builder, job tracker, and some keyword analysis features. Its strongest
differentiator is the job application tracker — keeping all your
applications, contacts, and notes in one place.

What it does well: job tracking and organization, keyword gap identification.

What it doesn't do: generate content for you, produce cover letters at
meaningful quality, or help with interview prep.

**Pricing:** Free plan (limited features), $29/month for Pro.

**Bottom line:** Good organizational layer if you're managing a high volume
of applications. The resume and optimization features are thinner than
dedicated tools.

---

### Kickresume

**Best for:** Visual resume design

Kickresume leans heavily into template quality and visual design. The
templates are genuinely attractive, and the platform has an AI writing
assistant that can help with individual sections.

What it does well: visual quality, template diversity, AI writing suggestions.

What it doesn't do: ATS-specific optimization, job strategy, interview prep.

**Pricing:** Free plan (limited templates), ~$19/month for premium.

**Bottom line:** If you're in a creative field where visual presentation
matters and ATS is less of a concern, Kickresume is worth considering.
